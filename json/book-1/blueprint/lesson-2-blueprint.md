# Lesson 2 - Everyday Objects

Level: A1 | Language: en

## Global Scope

This blueprint introduces everyday objects, possession with **have**, questions with **Do you have...?**, singular and plural objects, basic prices as useful shopping chunks, and the first controlled introduction to **DO / DOES**.

Keep the student-facing content in English.

Do not explain the verb **to be** in this lesson.

Do not teach **he has / she has / it has** yet.

For **does**, use only questions and short answers:

- Does he have...?
- Does she have...?
- Does it have...?
- Yes, he does.
- No, he doesn't.
- Yes, she does.
- No, she doesn't.
- Yes, it does.
- No, it doesn't.

Use everyday, simple, concrete situations.

**[Block: Heading - h1]**
Do You Have...?

**[Block: Paragraph]**
Style: intro
We use **Do you have...?** to ask about objects a person has.

**[Block: Advanced Grammar]** Basic Structure
Details: Use this structure to ask about things.

| Use                   | Pattern                   | Example            |
| --------------------- | ------------------------- | ------------------ |
| Question              | Do + you + have + object? | Do you have a pen? |
| Short Positive Answer | Yes, I do.                | Yes, I do.         |
| Short Negative Answer | No, I don't.              | No, I don't.       |

**[Block: Advanced Grammar]** More ways to answer
Explanation: Use a short answer first. Then you can add more information.
Details: Keep the answer simple.

| Question                | Short Answer | Long Answer                         |
| ----------------------- | ------------ | ----------------------------------- |
| Do you have a phone?    | Yes, I do.   | Yes, I do. I have a phone.          |
| Do you have a wallet?   | No, I don't. | No, I don't. I don't have a wallet. |
| Do you have headphones? | Yes, I do.   | Yes, I do. I have headphones.       |

**[Block: Flashcards]** Have and Don't Have

- Expressions: I have a pen.
  Meaning: Positive sentence.
  Back text: Use this when the object is with you.
- Expressions: I don't have a pen.
  Meaning: Negative sentence.
  Back text: Use this when the object is not with you.
- Expressions: Do you have a pen?
  Meaning: Question.
  Back text: Use this to ask another person.
- Expressions: Yes, I do.
  Meaning: Positive short answer.
  Back text: Use this when the answer is yes.
- Expressions: No, I don't.
  Meaning: Negative short answer.
  Back text: Use this when the answer is no.

DO or DOES?

**[Block: Paragraph]**
Style: intro
We use **DO** or **DOES** to make simple questions. The word changes with the person.

**[Block: Advanced Grammar]** People and Question Words
Details: In this lesson, use DOES only for questions and short answers.

| Person | Question Word | Example                 |
| ------ | ------------- | ----------------------- |
| I      | DO            | Do I have a pen?        |

| We     | DO            | Do we have notebooks?   |
| They   | DO            | Do they have backpacks? |
| He     | DOES          | Does he have a wallet?  |
| She    | DOES          | Does she have keys?     |
| It     | DOES          | Does it have a price?   |
